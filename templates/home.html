Introduction

Honeybees play a critical role in pollination, supporting biodiversity and agricultural productivity worldwide. Monitoring their population is essential for ecological research and sustainable agriculture. However, traditional methods of counting honeybees are labor-intensive and prone to errors.Our project leverages advanced deep learning techniques to automate honeybee detection and counting from images. Using models like YOLO, we have developed a robust system capable of detecting honeybees with high speed and accuracy. The web application allows users to upload images and instantly get the count of honeybees detected, along with annotated visuals.This innovative solution offers a scalable, efficient, and user-friendly way to monitor honeybee populations, contributing to ecological research and agricultural optimization efforts.

Problem Statement:

Monitoring honeybee populations is essential for ecological balance and agricultural sustainability. Traditional methods of counting honeybees are manual, time-consuming, and prone to errors, making it difficult to track population dynamics accurately.The lack of automated, scalable solutions for honeybee detection hinders efforts in ecological research, pollination management, and agricultural productivity. There is a pressing need for a reliable system that can accurately detect and count honeybees in diverse environments, minimizing human effort and improving monitoring efficiency.Our project addresses this challenge by leveraging deep learning models to develop an automated honeybee detection and counting system. Through this, we aim to provide a scalable and accurate solution for researchers, farmers, and ecologists.

Solution/Goals:

Our project aims to solve the challenges of manual honeybee monitoring by developing an automated system using deep learning.
  • Automate Detection: Use advanced object detection models to identify and count honeybees in images.
  • Compare Models: Evaluate YOLO and Faster R-CNN for accuracy and speed, selecting YOLO for deployment.
  • User-Friendly Access: Create a web application to upload images and display honeybee counts with bounding box annotations.
  • Support Research: Provide a scalable solution to aid ecological monitoring and agricultural productivity.
  • Future Adaptability: Design the system for scalability, with potential extensions to video analysis and multi-species monitoring.

Comparison

To identify the best model for honeybee detection, we trained and evaluated YOLOv8, YOLOv11, and Faster R-CNN on a custom dataset.

  • YOLOv8: Achieved the highest accuracy of 87.08%, offering the best balance of speed and precision.
  • YOLOv11: Delivered moderate performance with an accuracy of 63.02%, but fell short compared to YOLOv8.
  • Faster R-CNN: Demonstrated the lowest accuracy of 51.9%, primarily due to slower inference speed and challenges in detecting small objects like honeybees.
